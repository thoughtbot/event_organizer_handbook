This handbook grew out of three years organizing [RubyMX](https://comunidadruby.mx/en/home/), a Ruby meetup for the Mexican developer community. 
The lessons here are practical: what worked, what didn't, and what we'd do differently.

[Fernando Perales](https://thoughtbot.com/people/fernando-perales) has been part of the Mexican tech community for about ten years, starting as an attendee and 
workshop participant before becoming a presenter. He collaborated with the Mexican chapter of RailsBridge, and it was while serving as a teaching assistant at a 
RailsBridge workshop in Colima that he first crossed paths with thoughtbot, where he now works.

RubyMX started in March 2022 with one speaker from michelada.io, hosted at Bosch Connectory, a coworking space in Guadalajara that shared a building with 
michelada's offices. The first event had around five attendees, most of them friends. 
Today, RubyMX draws around 40 people in person and livestreams on [YouTube](https://www.youtube.com/@comunidadrubymx), where another ten or so usually watch.

Much of the time that went into organizing RubyMX, and into writing this handbook, happened during thoughtbot's [investment time](https://thoughtbot.com/blog/investment-time), a dedicated Friday 
each week for open source contributions, writing, and community work. thoughtbot has a long history in this space: the Boston office has hosted and organized 
meetups including [Boston.rb](https://thoughtbot.com/blog/bring-your-laptop-get-started-with-ruby-at-tuesdays) and [Design With Boston](https://thoughtbot.com/blog/introducing-design-with-boston), and the company has been involved with RailsBridge over the years.

Other thoughtbot members are running meetups in their own cities: [RubyDF](https://rubydf.com/) in Brazil by Matheus Richard and other one-off tech meetups
around the globe.

This handbook is published under the thoughtbot name. The experience behind it comes from RubyMX.
